About the workshop

The Rivet Community Workshop is a no-cost learning, collaboration, and networking event for people using the Rivet software design system. The event combines beginner-friendly lessons, curated tutorial projects, a showcase of example Rivet apps, and a catered social hour.

Registration is limited to 50 people. Registration will close after the first 50 people have registered.

Tutorial track

The tutorial track is a series of 25-minute lessons geared towards developers and designers new to Rivet. These lessons include an introduction to the design system itself, instructions and recommendations for contributing, and methods for customizing Rivet's built-in styles.

Each lesson will be held in Multipurpose Room B and will be led by Scott Murray, developer advocate and member of the Rivet core team. Multipurpose Room B can seat 16 people per lesson—seats will be assigned on a first-come, first-served basis.

Coding track

The coding track will be a place for people to work individually or in small groups on Rivet projects. Don't worry about creating something complete or perfect—the goal of the coding track is to help people get familiar with Rivet development and encourage them to think about issues like responsive design and accessbility.

The coding track will take place from 1:00-3:30 PM in the Wrubel Lobby. Levi McGranahan, design system lead, will be available to help people as they work through their projects.

We ask that you don't bring issues or bug reports to the workshop. If you're running into problems with your Rivet project, please file an issue on GitHub instead.
